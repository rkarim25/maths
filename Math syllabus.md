# Comprehensive 11+ Math Syllabus (Clean Step-by-Step)

## Step 1: Visual Mastery and Fluency
* **Number Bonds:** Absolute mastery of number bonds to 10, 20, and 100 without finger counting.
* **Place Value:** Understanding place value of two-digit numbers (e.g., recognizing 67 as 6 tens and 7 ones).
* **Skip Counting:** Skip counting fluently in 2s, 5s, and 10s, both forward and backward.

## Step 2: Operational Basics
* **Basic Arithmetic:** Two-digit addition and subtraction without regrouping.
* **Visual Regrouping:** Conceptual understanding of regrouping/carrying using concrete base-10 blocks (trading ten single ones for one ten-rod).
* **Early Multiplication & Division:** Understanding multiplication as "equal groups" and division as "fair sharing" or grouping.

## Step 3: Foundational Shapes and Fractions
* **Early Fractions:** Finding halves (1/2), quarters (1/4), and thirds (1/3) of shapes, objects, and small quantities.
* **Geometry:** Recognizing properties of 2D and 3D shapes (vertices, edges, faces, surfaces).
* **Basic Data:** Recording and reading data from simple pictograms, tally charts, and block diagrams.

## Step 4: Formal Written Mechanics
* **Expanded Place Value:** Mastering place value up to 1,000.
* **Column Methods:** Formal column addition and subtraction involving 3-digit numbers with carrying and borrowing/regrouping.

## Step 5: Introductory Multiplication Engine
* **Core Times Tables:** Rapid recall and automaticity of the 3, 4, and 8 times tables.
* **Written Multiplication:** Formal short multiplication methods, transitioning from the grid method to the standard column method.

## Step 6: Fractions Progression and Measurement
* **Fraction Arithmetic:** Identifying equivalent fractions and performing basic addition and subtraction of fractions with the same denominator.
* **Time Mastery:** Telling the time accurately on an analogue clock to the nearest minute, including converting between 12-hour and 24-hour clocks.
* **Practical Measurement:** Measuring, comparing, and adding/subtracting lengths (mm/cm/m), mass (g/kg), and volume/capacity (ml/l).

## Step 7: Advanced Whole Number Operations
* **Number Gymnastics:** Rounding numbers to the nearest 10, 100, or 1,000; understanding negative numbers in context; reading Roman numerals up to 100.
* **Formal Division:** Solving short division problems using the formal "bus stop" method, including handling simple remainders.

## Step 8: Complete Multiplication Automaticity and Decimals
* **The Grand Multiplication Goal:** Absolute automatic recall of all times tables up to 12 x 12 (critical for 11+ exam speed).
* **Decimal Intro:** Introduction to decimals (tenths and hundredths) and matching basic decimals to their fractional equivalents (e.g., 0.5 = 1/2, 0.25 = 1/4).

## Step 9: Geometry and Data Interpretation
* **Perimeter & Area:** Calculating the perimeter and area of regular and simple rectilinear shapes.
* **Angles & Lines:** Identifying acute, obtuse, and right angles; identifying parallel and perpendicular lines.
* **Data Handling:** Reading, interpreting, and constructing line graphs, bar charts, and coordinate grids in the first quadrant.

## Step 10: Advanced Arithmetic and Pre-Algebra
* **Order of Operations:** Applying BIDMAS/BODMAS rules correctly to multi-step equations.
* **Properties of Numbers:** Identifying prime numbers, factors, multiples, square numbers, and cube numbers.
* **FDP Conversions:** Seamlessly converting between fractions, decimals, and percentages (e.g., knowing that 0.75 = 75% = 3/4).
* **Proportional Reasoning:** Solving problems involving ratios, direct proportions, and simple scale factors.
* **Algebraic Thinking:** Introduction to basic algebraic equations, expressions, simplifying terms, and extending number sequences.

## Step 11: Complex Geometry and Advanced Data Representation
* **Logic Word Problems:** Solving multi-step mathematical logic problems and translating complex paragraph blocks into solvable equations.
* **Advanced Properties of Shapes:** Calculating missing angles inside triangles, quadrilaterals, on a straight line, and around a single point.
* **Spatial Transformation:** Executing and identifying geometric transformations including reflections, translations, and lines of symmetry.
* **Spatial Reasoning:** Visualizing 3D shape nets, counting hidden cubes, and spatial rotations (highly targeted in 11+ Non-Verbal sections).
* **Advanced Statistics:** Calculating mean, median, mode, and range; interpreting complex pie charts, Venn diagrams, and Carroll diagrams.

## Step 12: Exam Mechanics and Test Strategy
* **Pacing & Speed:** Engaging in targeted speed and accuracy drills, aiming to spend an average of 45 seconds per question.
* **Multiple Choice Tactics:** Mastering elimination strategies and process-of-elimination techniques tailored for formal answer sheets.
* **Mock Analysis:** Sitting regular, timed full-length mock papers and utilizing error analysis to systematically isolate and correct topic gaps.
