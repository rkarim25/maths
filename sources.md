# Curated 11+ Mathematics Curriculum Resources

This document compiles the absolute gold-standard UK curriculum resources, books, and digital frameworks recommended for your daughter's 4-year learning path (from Year 2 through to the 11+ exams in September 2030). 

By feeding these descriptions, structures, and methodologies into **NotebookLM** or your **Gemini Workspace**, you establish a rigorous "master brain" that enables your AI to generate highly targeted interactive code, precise difficulty scaling, and mathematically sound lesson scripts.

---

## 1. Foundational Concept Mastery (Ages 6–9 / Years 2–4)
The focus during this early stage is building deep conceptual understanding and rock-solid number sense using the **Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract (CPA)** framework. These resources will guide your AI prompts to structure visual elements (like virtual ten-frames and Base-10 drag-and-drop counters) before introducing abstract written equations.

### A. "Maths — No Problem!" (Year 2 to Year 4 Textbook Series)
* **What it is:** The UK's premier textbook series based on the highly successful Singapore Math method.
* **Core Purpose:** To master visual and spatial mapping using the CPA framework.
* **AI Implementation:** Use descriptions of this methodology to prompt Gemini/NotebookLM to design interactive visual aids. Analyze how these books introduce concepts via visual bar models, block diagrams, and visual counter groupings before transitioning to pure abstract numbers.

### B. Schofield & Sims: Mental Arithmetic (Introductory Books & Books 1–3)
* **What it is:** The definitive primary school standard for rapid-fire number sense and numerical automaticity.
* **Core Purpose:** To build lightning-fast, finger-free mental arithmetic accuracy and numerical gymnastics.
* **AI Implementation:** Use the classic, structured question types from these books as structural templates for your backend `questions.json` files on your static GitHub Pages site.

### C. CGP 11+ Foundations: Maths (Ages 7–8 / Year 3)
* **What it is:** A gentler, foundational tier of the well-known CGP exam preparation books.
* **Core Purpose:** To bridge the gap between normal classroom arithmetic and the early logical thinking patterns required for selective school entrance exams.
* **AI Implementation:** Feed this structure into your AI to generate early problem-solving drills that introduce reasoning principles without causing cognitive burnout.

## 2. The 11+ Heavyweights (Ages 8–10 / Years 4–5)
As the learning plan pivots from conceptual mastery into exam strategy, these resources provide the industry-standard formats for GL Assessment and CEM exam styles.

### A. Bond 11+ Maths: 10-Minute Tests (Ages 8–9, 9–10, and 10–11)
* **What it is:** The oldest, most trusted, and benchmarked name in 11+ preparation.
* **Core Purpose:** To establish a daily micro-habit of testing and provide an accurate difficulty benchmark.
* **AI Implementation:** Use these books to calibrate your AI's difficulty scaling. For example, you can prompt Gemini: *"Generate 10 word problems at a Bond 11+ Age 9–10 difficulty rating centered around multi-step operations."*

### B. CGP 11+ GL / CEM Maths Practice Books & Assessment Tests
* **What it is:** Highly popular, clear, and engaging test workbooks designed specifically for the core 11+ exam boards.
* **Core Purpose:** To provide rigorous exam preparation through a gamified, lighthearted layout that injects subtle humor and fun puzzle pages.
* **AI Implementation:** Use this framework to prompt your AI for engaging, narrative-driven math stories (e.g., matching your daughter's interests) to keep anxiety low and maintain high interest.

### C. GL Assessment Official 11+ Practice Papers
* **What it is:** Authentic past papers and mock exams from the primary grammar school examination board.
* **Core Purpose:** To transition completely to physical paper practice during the final Year 5 stretch to master exam-room mechanics.
* **AI Implementation:** While keeping the papers physical, use your AI to analyze the specific topic gaps revealed by her mock scores, feeding the problem areas back into your digital platform for targeted revision. Use these to teach bubble-sheet filling, time management, and process of elimination.

## 3. Free Digital Frameworks (For AI Knowledge Base & GitHub Structure)
These open-source materials can be downloaded as free text or PDF files and uploaded directly into your AI workspace as primary documents to map out your software architecture.

### A. White Rose Maths (Schemes of Learning)
* **Source:** Available as free parental/teacher downloads at `whiterosemaths.com`.
* **Core Purpose:** This represents the exact structural curriculum map used by the vast majority of UK primary schools. 
* **AI Implementation:** Download the Year 2 and Year 3 "Term Overviews." These files show precisely how mathematical concepts break down into small micro-steps. Drop these PDFs straight into NotebookLM to map your GitHub Pages site directory (e.g., `/year2/module1/`).

### B. The UK National Curriculum: Mathematics Appendix
* **Source:** Official UK Government education portal (`GOV.UK`).
* **Core Purpose:** Outlines every regulatory mathematical expectation for Key Stage 1 (KS1) and Key Stage 2 (KS2).
* **AI Implementation:** Use this text file inside Gemini to cross-reference and verify that your custom-coded platform covers all mandatory academic baselines.

## Pro-Tip for NotebookLM Knowledge Ingestion
You do not need to manually transcribe text from physical prep books. To teach your AI model to match a specific publisher's tone or pedagogical explanation style:
1. Take a clear photograph of an exceptionally clear explanation layout or a well-framed multi-step word problem using your phone.
2. Save or compile the image as a PDF or clear text note.
3. Upload it as a primary source inside your synced Gemini Notebook or NotebookLM workspace.
4. Prompt the model: *"Analyze the tone and micro-step breakdown of this uploaded resource, and adapt your subsequent math question generation to mirror this exact style."*
